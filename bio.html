Spotlight: Kellie Larson

Kellie Larson is a commercially potent, cinematic storyteller specializing in Story-First songwriting built on "Visceral Specificity," where she expertly crafts high-impact emotional narratives that master the Empowerment Arc—the transformative journey from vulnerability to definitive agency—while simultaneously dominating the Country-Folk Noir niche through Actionable Catharsis to translate complex relational conflict into immediate, righteous confrontation. Across her 44-song portfolio, she bridges the gap between modern digital betrayal and timeless character-driven storytelling through four distinct creative pillars—Anthemic Power, Raw Emotional Landscapes, Cinematic Niche, and Commercial Hooks—ensuring every track functions as a GPS-ready asset for sync placement and Nashville-standard publishing, all backed by the strategic business rigor of her 25-year tenure as a high-level Executive Assistant.

Pillar I: Anthemic Power & Defiance

Big Hard No

Concept: A fierce, definitive response to betrayal that moves from reflective hurt to absolute independence.

Placement: Empowerment trailers or dramatic "walk-away" scenes.

"Nothing you could whisper can ever douse this flame / You can't mess with my day, no way, not anymore!"

Broken Record

Concept: A raw anthem of self-reclamation, moving from bitter defeat to breaking free from a one-sided relationship.

Placement: Gritty film/TV scenes focused on emotional breakthroughs.

"I'm a broken record, skipping on repeat / This ain't a cry for help, it's the bitter taste of defeat."

CEO Not Babysitting Tree • Hit the Road • My Girl Karma • The Back of Me • Don't Want You Back

Pillar II: Raw Emotional Landscapes

Etched in Every Space

Concept: A devastating look at the physical presence of absence and the suffocating pain of irreplaceable loss.

Placement: Soulful, career-defining ballads for major artists or prestige drama sync.

"Oh, I miss your stupid face! It's etched in every space / When I called out your name, you always came."

The Aftermath

Concept: Exploring the "suffocating quiet" and internal turmoil following a destructive, toxic relationship.

Placement: Indie/alternative films featuring high-stakes emotional reflection.

"I used to crave that familiar ache, the twisted bind / Found bizarre comfort in the chaos left behind."

Take That First Step • Fragile • Messy • Rain • Your Mask • Solid Shifting Ground • Ephemeral Moment

Pillar III: Cinematic Niche & Country-Folk Noir

Murderer's Heart

Concept: A Southern Gothic tale of cold justice and narrative twists using "Daddy's Colt .22".

Placement: Thriller trailers or period pieces focused on psychological suspense.

"He thought he was safe, he thought he could outsmart / But he was playing games with a murderer's heart."

Welcome to Jukes

Concept: An atmospheric "world-builder" that shifts from a lively bayou vibe to foreboding horror.

Placement: Opening credits for detective series or Southern Gothic horror.

"Step into the rhythm where the neon bleeds... Once the doors are locked, no one leaves."

Wildflower • Fury (I'm the Monster) • Children of War • One Small Wrong • Shadow Walk
