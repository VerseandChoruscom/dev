BOTTOM OF THE WISHING WELL

Genre: Dark Folk / Murder Ballad | Mood: Tense, Macabre | Placement: Cinematic Thriller / Dark Americana
Verse 1
He led her down to the edge of the woods,
Promised her things that he never could.
A ring for her finger, a home on the hill,
But his heart was cold, and his blood was still.
They reached the stone circle where the shadows dwell,
Right at the lip of the old wishing well.
Chorus
Oh, careful what you wish for in the dark of the night,
The stars are all watching, but they have no light.
The water is deep and the secrets don't tell,
What lies at the bottom of the wishing well.
Yeah, the silver is tarnished and the copper is cold,
Down at the bottom where the stories unfold.
Verse 2
He told her to lean in and make one more plea,
"Wish for a lifetime with a man just like me."
As she closed her eyes, he felt for the knife,
Ready to trade her young soul for his life.
But she felt the shift in the air and the sound,
And as he lunged forward, she spun him around.
He didn't expect she would grip with such a mighty hold.
Outro
He didn't trip, nothing could loosen her grip,
She’s stuck like glue, and both their wishes came true.
Oh, what a mess, they lay motionless,
At the bottom of the wishing well.
